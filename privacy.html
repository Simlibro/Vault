Privacy and security

What Vault does, and what it does not do

Vault is built around a simple model. Your vault is encrypted locally on your device using your master password. There is no built-in online account, no remote password database, and no automatic server-side recovery.

What Vault does

  • Encrypts data locally in the browser on your device.
  • Stores working data locally unless you deliberately export it.
  • Lets you move encrypted backups manually between devices when you choose.

What Vault does not do

  • No automatic sync between devices.
  • No developer recovery of a forgotten master password.
  • No protection from an already compromised device or poor device hygiene.

Your responsibilities

Choose a strong master password. Keep encrypted backups. Be careful when moving vault files between devices. Treat the newest exported copy as important.

Important limitation

If you forget your master password, the developer cannot recover it for you. If you lose your local data and do not have a backup, it may be gone permanently.

Plain answer

Vault is designed to keep your passwords under your control. That means less hidden infrastructure, but it also means more responsibility stays with the user.

Vault reduces trust in third-party infrastructure, but it does not remove the need for strong passwords, device security, and backups.
