Offline by design

Vault is a password manager that runs on your device, not on someone else’s server.

Vault is a small local-first password manager PWA. Your vault is encrypted in the browser on your own device using your master password. There is no built-in account system, no automatic cloud sync, and no remote password database managed by the developer.

At a glance

  • Encrypted locally before storage on your device.
  • No cloud account and no hosted vault service.
  • Manual export and import when you want to move an encrypted vault between devices.

Why use Vault

Vault is for people who prefer a simple trust model. Open locally, encrypt locally, store locally, and back up deliberately.

The trade-off is responsibility. You must keep backups, remember your master password, and manage which exported copy is the newest.

What it stores

Vault stores titles, usernames, passwords, recovery notes, URLs, and general notes in one encrypted local vault.

See the Install page for platform guidance, the Privacy page for responsibilities, and the Technical page for implementation detail.

Plain summary

Vault does not send your passwords to a Vault server. The developer cannot recover a forgotten master password. Your data stays under your control, which also means backup discipline is your responsibility.

Vault is designed to work in a browser and as an installed PWA.
